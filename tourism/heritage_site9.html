The Roman Agora

Description: The Roman Agora is a large public space located to the north of the Ancient Agora of Athens. It was developed during the Roman period and features several important buildings and structures.

History: Constructed during the 1st century BC and 1st century AD, the Roman Agora included a marketplace, public baths, and a large colonnaded courtyard. Key structures include the Gate of Athena Archegetis and the Tower of the Winds, a unique octagonal structure that served as a clocktower and meteorological station.

The Roman Agora reflects the influence of Roman architecture and urban planning on Athens, showcasing a blend of Greek and Roman cultural elements.
