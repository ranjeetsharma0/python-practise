The Temple of Olympian Zeus

Description: The Temple of Olympian Zeus, also known as the Olympieion, is a colossal ancient temple dedicated to Zeus, the king of the Greek gods.

History: Construction of the temple began in the 6th century BC under the tyrant Peisistratus and was completed in the 2nd century AD by the Roman emperor Hadrian. The temple originally consisted of 104 Corinthian columns, of which 15 still stand today. It was the largest temple in Greece and was intended to rival the Parthenon in grandeur.

The site was also associated with various religious festivals and ceremonies in honor of Zeus. Despite its incomplete state, the remains of the temple continue to impress visitors with its sheer scale and architectural innovation.
